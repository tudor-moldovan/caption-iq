Case studies

The real work.

Client names are confidential. The problems and the results are not.

Communications · Enterprise

From biweekly releases to weekly — without more testers

The situation

A 200-person SaaS company had manual regression eating 5 days of every release cycle. Releases slipped monthly. Developers were writing code faster than QA could validate it.

What I did

Mapped every regression test to business risk. Automated the top 40% by execution frequency using Playwright. Built go/no-go criteria so the team could make release decisions in hours, not days.

Result

Regression cycle cut by more than half. Release cadence moved from biweekly to weekly. No additional headcount required.

Fintech · Scale-up

Building a QA function from zero at a Series A startup

The situation

A fintech startup with 25 engineers had shipped without any formal QA. Their first major enterprise client required a documented test strategy and sign-off process before going live.

What I did

Wrote the QA strategy from scratch, defined coverage targets, hired and onboarded two QA engineers, set up the bug tracking workflow, and owned the first release sign-off end-to-end.

Result

The enterprise contract was signed. First release under the new QA process shipped on time with zero critical defects in production.

Technology · Enterprise

QA consistency across three concurrent product launches

The situation

A technology group was launching three separate products in the same quarter, each with its own dev team and no shared quality standards. Each team defined "done" differently.

What I did

Defined cross-product quality standards and acceptance criteria. Managed a QA team of 8 testers distributed across all three products. Owned release sign-off for all three launches.

Result

All three products launched on schedule. Consistent quality standards adopted across teams and carried into future releases.
